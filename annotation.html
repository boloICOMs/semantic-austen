Annotation Campaign

Task Definition

We decided to do an annotation campaign on some of Jane Austen’s manuscripts, we took three texts from different moments in her life to create an homogeneous batch of texts that cover her lifetime as an author. For the content of the manuscripts we took the diplomatic transcriptions done in the Scholarly Digital Edition Jane Austen fiction Manuscripts.
Our aim is to create a Model that can generate an automatic transcription of all Jane Austen’s manuscripts in our theoretical Semantic Digital Library Semantic Austen that includes all the work done in the existing SDE. Therefore we create this model on Austen handwriting so any other existing holograph texts of her can be transcribed using our model.

Context

Jane Austen (1775-1817) was an English writer during the English romanticism of the 19th century. Remembered as one of the most important female authors of English literature, known primarily for her novels that explore topics of marriage, social status and economic situation of women through the female perspective of the English society of the 18-19 century.
Just some of her manuscripts survive the pace of time and are in possession of several english libraries that digitize them and allow a visualization of them.


We chose to work on three of her surviving manuscripts available online.

  • Juvenilia: is a collection of stories that she wrote in the span of six years (1787-1793). The first volume of the manuscript is property of the Bodleian Library, Oxford that digitalized and allows a visualization and download of the images.

  • The Watsons: is an unfinished work written around 1803 , and probably abandoned after Austen’s father. The incomplete work is divided in two parts, the second is property of the Bodleian Library, Oxford that digitalized and provides the visualization and download of the images.

  • Persuasion: her latest novel written before her death; it was published postmortem in 1817. The only surviving holographs of the novel are the first two chapters that are property of the British Library that digitalized and provided visualization of the manuscript.


The images of the Bodleian Library, Oxford are under the CC-BY-NC license and the British Library are of public domain


The Scholarly Digital Edition (SDE) done by King’s College and Oxford called Jane Austen fiction Manuscripts. Where they made available the images of the digitized surviving manuscripts in possessions of the several institutions and made an extensive philological work of the manuscripts while providing the diplomatic transcriptions of the texts.
For the annotation of the manuscripts we use the diplomatic transcriptions included on the SDE as ground truth.


Although the SDE is a very good digital edition with extensive work, it is slightly outdated and does not make use of semantic and interoperable elements. Hence we would like to propose to make this Scholarly Digital Edition become a Semantic Digital Library called Semantic Austen.

Where we consider all the work done in the SDE and take it forward, providing the images of the manuscripts with IIIF, the text of the manuscripts to be downloadable in XML, metadata in LOD with the link to the authority files of her and the characters in the novel. In addition to providing external links related to Jane Auste, her novel and her life's work to render this highly respected scholarly endeavor a semantic and state of the art website.

Corpus Selection

As mentioned before we select three texts; Juvenilia, The Watsons, Persuasion that are representative of different periods of Austen’s work and handwriting (from childhood to maturity). To train the model on different ways of her handwriting since it changes in the lifespan of authors, hence our aim is to create a model that is able to transcribe all the existing Jane Austen manuscripts included in our potential SDL and any others that are in the word. Therefore we decided to not use a pre-existing english HTR model for the training of ours since we wanted to create from scratch from only Austen holographs and for just her writings.

We began with the annotation of 50 pages between the three manuscripts, later we decided to expand it to make the corpus bigger to make the model work better.
The final corpus was 68 annotated pages between the three texts ( 28 pages of Juvenilia, 25 pages of The Watsons and 15 pages of Persuasion).

Annotation Guidelines

Our campaign acts in accordance with the OCR and Transkribus guidelines.

Furthermore to carry out the annotation campaign, we relied as said above on the diplomatic transcription of the SDE for the transcription of the texts. This aligns with level 2 of the OCR-D Guidelines for Ground Truth as their objective is to preserve original text as accurately as possible, like stated in the SDE as well.

Since the texts in the manuscripts follow a certain logic we defined some internal guidelines for special cases often encountered. We divided in layout and text guidelines.

Layout Guidelines

We defined custom made regions to distinguish structural elements of the page:


  • Text: region that covers main part of the page, is the actual content of the texts (Imagine)
  • Title: used in Juvenilia since is a collection of different of stories (imagine)
  • Chapter : used in Juvenilia and Persuasion
  • Page-number: used in Juvenilia (imagine)
  • Section number: used in The Watson since she wrote on separate numbered quires (imagine)
  • Marginalia: used in Persuasion in some pages she include dates (day and month) (imagine)
  • All lines traced on the page that are not a baseline of text were ignored

Text Guidelines

The handwritten text conveyed particularities that had to be specified manually,such as corrections, additions made by the author, we normalized normalize like they did the SDE.


  • Erasures with a correction on top we defined an extra baseline for the correction and tagged the erased text as strikethrough while we tag the correct text on top as superscript
  • Additions of text (^) defined in an extra baseline and tag as superscript and described with the custom tag addition
  • Erased text on top of which is a correction pointed out as addition by ^, we only tag the correction as superscript
  • Didn’t include (^); expressed with the addition tag
  • Letters written with pencil and later erased; weren’t transcribed
  • We follow diplomatic transcription in punctuation
  • Mʳ and similars normalized into Mr

Annotation Platform

We used for our annotation campaign the platform Transkribus, that allows us to upload the images of the manuscript from the Bodleian Library and the British Library and carry out our annotation and the training of our HTR Model.

Pilot

The pilot was performed thinking about how efficient our model was, since we created it from scratch and did not take an already pre-existing English HTR model. Using 50 pages between the three novels, made us understand that our annotation process was corrected yet we had to make the data-set bigger to receive the results that we expected.

Campaign

The full campaign involved a team of six expert annotators over a period of three months. More than 400 manuscript pages were transcribed and verified, creating a gold-standard dataset for the training of our semantic models.

Conclusions

The results demonstrate that while AI models are becoming increasingly proficient at basic transcription, the preservation of complex layout features and diplomatic nuances still requires human expertise. Our hybrid approach ensures both scale and academic precision for the Semantic Austen collection.
